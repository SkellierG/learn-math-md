## Motivations
Recently, when I decided to relearn mathematics, I was driven by a feeling of inadequacy, as I was struggling to understand secondary education topics. In this process of rediscovery, I was amazed at how incredibly logical and natural everything felt. As explanations broke down from fundamental principles and the reasons behind each concept were clarified, I began to experience mathematics in a completely new way, feeling it as intuitive and natural as the natural numbers to a child learning to count.

I am deeply grateful to the teachers who share their passion for teaching on YouTube, conveying their enthusiasm for learning and discovery. Although my knowledge is still developing, I can confidently say that I meet the academic requirements of my educational level and that I will continue to expand my knowledge in the future. With pride, I can affirm that I do this out of true passion; these teachers and other sources have ignited in me a profound love for mathematics and an urgent need to understand it thoroughly.

In this project, I am testing and experimenting with my own understanding, and I hope you will do the same.

Mathematics are not simply arbitrary rules or tricks like PEMDAS; they are much more than that. They exist because brilliant minds, through long periods of reflection and work, have developed this knowledge throughout history. Mathematics is not just about knowing it, but about learning it deeply. That’s why I created this GitHub repository: I felt the need to provide easy access to the knowledge of this wonderful discipline. I have established a learning path so that not only is there a mathematical encyclopedia, like Wikipedia, but also specific problems within the repository with didactic explanations and a predefined path to follow.

I hope that my friends, who sometimes struggle with mathematics, even in "basic" concepts, will be able to advance thanks to a true and deep understanding of this subject.

## How to contribute

Thank you for your interest in contributing to this project! There are several ways you can help improve this mathematics encyclopedia:

### 1. Report Errors

If you find any typos, mathematical errors, or any other kind of mistake, please open an [issue](https://github.com/SkellierG/learn-math-md/issues) describing the problem in detail. Be sure to include any relevant information that could help reproduce or identify the issue.

### 2. Suggest Improvements

If you have ideas for improving the content, adding new sections, or suggesting structural changes, open an [issue](https://github.com/SkellierG/learn-math-md/issues) to discuss your proposal. This way, we can have a discussion about the best way to implement it.

### 3. Contribute Content

To contribute content directly:

1. **Fork** this repository.
2. **Clone** your fork to your local machine.
3. **Create a new branch** for your contribution: `git checkout -b your-branch-name`.
4. Make your changes to the Markdown files using a text editor or your preferred editor.
5. **Add problems and solutions**:
   - Include problems to solve for each topic to enhance the educational content.
   - Provide clear and concise solutions or hints to each problem in the corresponding section.
6. **Add sources and citations** where necessary:
   - **Avoid plagiarism** by ensuring that all content is original or properly cited.
   - **Search for and add sources** if you notice that previous content lacks proper citations or references. Be sure to include full links or bibliographic references.
7. **Commit** your changes with a clear and descriptive message: `git commit -m "Description of your contribution"`.
8. **Push** your branch: `git push origin your-branch-name`.
9. Open a **Pull Request** from your branch in your fork to the `main` branch of this repository.

### 4. Review of Pull Requests

All contributions will be reviewed by the project maintainers. Make sure to maintain a similar writing style to ensure a quicker review process. Maintainers may request changes before your contribution is merged.

### 5. Requirements and Style Guidelines

- Use Markdown for all content.
- Follow standard mathematical notation rules and ensure all formulas and equations are correct and well-formatted.
- Keep the language clear and concise, suitable for an educational audience.
- **Include problems** for each topic to provide practical exercises for readers.
- **Provide solutions or hints** for each problem to facilitate understanding and learning.
- **Include citations and references** when using material from other sources, following appropriate citation standards.

### 6. Communication

Join our discussions in the `WIP` section to suggest ideas, ask questions, or collaborate with others.

Thank you for helping to improve this mathematics encyclopedia!

## Motivaciones
Recientemente, cuando decidí reaprender matemáticas, lo hice impulsado por un sentimiento de insuficiencia, ya que estaba teniendo dificultades para entender los temas de la educación secundaria. En este proyecto, me estoy poniendo a prueba y experimentando con mi propio entendimiento, y espero que tú también lo hagas.

## Cómo Contribuir

¡Gracias por tu interés en contribuir a este proyecto! Hay varias formas en que puedes ayudar a mejorar esta enciclopedia matemática:

### 1. Reportar Errores

Si encuentras errores tipográficos, errores matemáticos o cualquier otro tipo de error, por favor abre un [issue](https://github.com/SkellierG/learn-math-md/issues) describiendo el problema en detalle. Asegúrate de incluir cualquier información relevante que pueda ayudar a reproducir o identificar el problema.

### 2. Sugerir Mejoras

Si tienes ideas para mejorar el contenido, agregar nuevas secciones o sugerir cambios estructurales, abre un [issue](https://github.com/SkellierG/learn-math-md/issues) para discutir tu propuesta. De esta manera, podremos tener una discusión sobre la mejor manera de implementarla.

### 3. Contribuir Contenido

Para contribuir contenido directamente:

1. **Haz un fork** de este repositorio.
2. **Clona** tu fork a tu máquina local.
3. **Crea una nueva rama** para tu contribución: `git checkout -b nombre-de-tu-rama`.
4. Realiza tus cambios en los archivos Markdown utilizando un editor de texto o tu editor preferido.
5. **Añade problemas y soluciones**:
   - Incluye problemas para resolver en cada tema para mejorar el contenido educativo.
   - Proporciona soluciones claras y concisas o pistas para cada problema en la sección correspondiente.
6. **Añade fuentes y citas** cuando sea necesario:
   - **Evita el plagio** asegurándote de que todo el contenido sea original o esté correctamente citado.
   - **Busca y añade fuentes** si notas que el contenido anterior carece de citas o referencias adecuadas. Asegúrate de incluir enlaces completos o referencias bibliográficas.
7. **Haz un commit** de tus cambios con un mensaje claro y descriptivo: `git commit -m "Descripción de tu contribución"`.
8. **Sube** tu rama: `git push origin nombre-de-tu-rama`.
9. Abre un **Pull Request** desde tu rama en tu fork hacia la rama `main` de este repositorio.

### 4. Revisión de Pull Requests

Todas las contribuciones serán revisadas por los desarrolladores del proyecto. Asegúrate de mantener un estilo de escritura similar para facilitar un proceso de revisión más rápido. Los desarrolladores pueden solicitar cambios antes de que tu contribución sea fusionada.

### 5. Requisitos y Directrices de Estilo

- Usa Markdown para todo el contenido.
- Sigue las reglas estándar de notación matemática y asegura que todas las fórmulas y ecuaciones sean correctas y estén bien formateadas.
- Mantén el lenguaje claro y conciso, adecuado para una audiencia educativa.
- **Incluye problemas** para cada tema para proporcionar ejercicios prácticos a los lectores.
- **Proporciona soluciones o pistas** para cada problema para facilitar la comprensión y el aprendizaje.
- **Incluye citas y referencias** al usar material de otras fuentes, siguiendo los estándares de citación apropiados.

### 6. Comunicación

Únete a nuestras discusiones en la sección `WIP` para sugerir ideas, hacer preguntas o colaborar con otros.
